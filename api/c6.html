c6 – implementation of C6 metric¶

cockatoo.c6.bare_bones(cocktail1, cocktail2, compute=True)[source]¶

Implementation of the “bare bones” metric from C6 (Newman et al. 2010).

This function implements a modified Canberra metric to compute the distance between two cocktails based on the concentration of their compounds.

\[D(a,b) = \frac{1}{T}\sum\limits_{t=1}^T \frac{|[a_t]-[b_t]|}{max[t]} \]

Where \(T\) is the number of distinct compounds in cocktails \(a\) and \(b\), \([a_t]\) is the concentration of compound \(t\) in cocktail \(a\), and \(max[t]\) is the maximum concentration for compound \(t\) across all known screens

Parameters:
  • cocktail1 (cocktail) – First cocktail to compare
  • cocktail2 (cocktail) – Second cocktail to compare
Returns:

The distance score between 0 and 1
cockatoo.c6.distance(cocktail1, cocktail2)[source]¶

Implementation of the “extended” metric from C6 (Newman et al. 2010). Incorporates pH, ion, and PEG factors.

\[\begin{split}\begin{eqnarray} D_{ij} & = & \frac{1}{T+3}\Biggl(\biggl(\Bigl(\sum_{t=1}^{T}\frac{\left|\left[s_{ti}\right]-\left[s_{tj}\right]\right|}{\max\left[s_{t}\right]}\Bigr)+\Bigl(\frac{\left|E(pH_{i})-E(pH_{j})\right|}{gul(pH)-gll(pH)}\Bigr)\biggr) \\ & + & \min\left(1,\left[\left(\frac{\left|\left[ion_{i}\right]-\left[ion_{j}\right]\right|}{\frac{\left(\max\left[ion_{i}\right]+\max\left[ion_{j}\right]\right)}{2}}\right)+0.3\right]\right) \\ & + & \min\left(1,\left[\left(\frac{\left|\left[PEG_{i}\right]-\left[PEG_{j}\right]\right|}{\frac{\left(\max\left[PEG_{i}\right]+\max\left[PEG_{j}\right]\right)}{2}}\right)+0.2\right]\right)\Biggr) \end{eqnarray}\end{split}\]

Where \(T\) is the number of distinct chemical species in conditions \(i\) and \(j\), \([s_{ti}]\) is the concentration of chemical \(t\) in condition \(i\), \(max[s_t]\) is the maximum concentration found for chemical \(t\), \(E(pH_i)\) is an estimate of the pH for condition \(i\), \(gul(pH)\) is the overall maximum of pH, \(gll(pH)\) is the overall minimum pH, \([ion_i]\) is the concentration of the salt \(i\), and \([PEG_i]\) is the concentration of PEG \(i\).

Parameters:
  • cocktail1 (cocktail) – First cocktail to compare
  • cocktail2 (cocktail) – Second cocktail to compare
Returns:

The distance score between 0 and 1
